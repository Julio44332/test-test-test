High-Wing Trainer

Perfect for beginners, these planes are stable and easy to fly. Their high-wing design offers great lift and self-correction, making them very forgiving.

RC Glider

Known for their long flight times, gliders rely on air currents to stay aloft. They are great for a calm, relaxing flying experience in wide open spaces.

RC Jet

Fast and exhilarating, RC jets are for experienced pilots. They are a true test of skill, capable of incredible speeds and complex aerobatic maneuvers.

Common Questions & Fixes

Q: Why does my plane fly in circles?

A: This is often caused by a trim issue. Adjust your rudder trim on the controller to counteract the turn until the plane flies straight. A slight wing warp can also cause this.

Q: My plane won't take off, what's wrong?

A: Check if the propeller is on correctly (spinning in the right direction) and if the battery is fully charged. Ensure you have enough runway to gain speed.

Q: The controller isn't connecting to the plane.

A: Make sure both the plane and controller are turned on and the plane's battery is connected first. Ensure they are properly "bound" according to the manufacturer's instructions. Keep them close during the binding process.

Q: The plane suddenly stops responding in the air.

A: This can be a range issue or a low battery on either the plane or the controller. Always fly within your radio's safe range and check your batteries before every flight.

Q: The motor is making a weird clicking sound.

A: This often indicates a gear issue in the motor or a piece of debris stuck in the propeller. Stop the motor immediately and inspect the gears and prop for any damage or obstructions.

Q: I've crashed. Is it a total loss?

A: Not always! Most foam planes can be fixed with CA glue. A broken propeller or a dislodged battery are common and easy fixes. Inspect the fuselage for cracks and the wings for damage first.
